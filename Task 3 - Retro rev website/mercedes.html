Mercedes-Benz 190E

The Mercedes-Benz 190E, introduced in the early 1980s, set new standards for compact luxury and performance. Built with Mercedes’ legendary engineering, it featured a robust design, advanced aerodynamics, and a well-appointed interior. The high-performance 190E 2.3-16 and later 2.5-16 models, developed with input from Cosworth, boasted a high-revving four-cylinder engine, producing up to 235 horsepower in its Evo II variant.

Known for its durability, precise handling, and racing pedigree—including success in the DTM touring car series—the 190E remains a cherished classic, embodying the perfect blend of luxury and motorsport heritage.
